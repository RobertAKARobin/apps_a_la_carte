If it's your first time building an app, or you've only worked with freelancers and overseas developers, it might!

This estimate is based on my experience as a “Solutions Architect” for a mid-size development shop based in the US and Bulgaria, called MentorMate.

How much your app actually costs may be more, or may be less. Things change based on the specific features you want, and even more based on how much risk you're willing to take.

What is “risk”?

It's a measure of how sure you can be that you'll get a functioning, finished product. An established development company with a lot of customers and experience is low-risk. Their development will include quality assurance testing and project management because they have a brand to protect. This may cost more in the short-term, but should make up for it in the long-term because you won't need to spend so much fixing bugs, and a better product will bring in more revenue.

On the other hand, hiring a freelancer, a technical co-founder, or an offshore development company is high-risk. They may cost less in the short-term, but may also cost much more in the long-term because you don't have the “safety net” of a team of people all working to manage your development experience. Making an app is a complicated process, and a lack of accountability and experience can quickly spiral into a storm of bugs and un-met promises.

What balance of risk and cost is best? That's up to you!
