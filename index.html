Seem like a lot?

If it's your first time building an app, or you've only worked with freelancers and overseas developers, it might!

In app development, you get what you pay for.

This estimate is based on our experience at a mid-size development shop based in the US and Bulgaria.

How much your app actually costs may be more, or may be less. Things change based on the specific features you want, and even more based on how much risk you're willing to take.

What is “risk”?

It's a measure of how sure you can be that you'll get a functioning, finished product.

Working with an established development company like MentorMate, which has a lot of customers and experience, is low-risk.

Our development includes quality assurance testing and project management because because we've worked hard to build our brand and want to protect it.

A well-managed product will bring in more revenue, and leave you free to grow your business instead of worrying about bugs.

On the other hand, hiring a freelancer, a technical co-founder, or an offshore development company is high-risk.

They may cost less in the short-term, but may also cost much more in the long-term because you don't have the “safety net” of a team of people all working to manage your development experience.

Making an app is a complicated process, and a lack of accountability and experience can quickly spiral into a storm of bugs and un-met promises.

What balance of risk and cost is best? That's up to you!
